💼 Career & Job · Public Question
What documents and procedures are required to apply for a position at a Korean government research institute?
I completed my PhD in Materials Engineering in Germany and am planning to apply to an NST-affiliated government research institute in Korea. Could you walk me through the entire process from document preparation to final acceptance, and let me know if there are any special requirements for foreign researchers? I'm particularly curious about the document review, interview format, and language requirements.

The recruitment process at NST-affiliated government research institutes typically follows this sequence: Document Review → Presentation Interview → Executive Interview → Health Check → Final Acceptance.


Documents to prepare: Résumé (in English), research publication list (SCI/SCIE papers), notarized copy of PhD diploma, 2 recommendation letters (from department head or advisor), research plan, and language proficiency scores (if required). Foreign documents require either an Apostille certification or notarization in Korea.


Language requirements: Most institutes accept applicants who can communicate in English. Korean proficiency is not mandatory, but holding a TOPIK score may give you bonus points.
